Session 1.4
Presentation Skills & Professional Presence
How we show up, how we communicate, and the core consultative skills that define great PSC
▾
A great demo delivered by someone who looks disengaged, speaks over the prospect, or doesn't actively listen isn't a great demo. This session is about the professional presence and consultative skills that sit underneath everything else we're building this week — how you show up physically and virtually, how you make people feel heard, and how the small things (camera on, eye contact, body language, genuine curiosity) determine whether a prospect trusts you enough to share what they really need. We'll cover virtual presence, in-person presence, active listening, and how to ask better questions.
Come ready to
  • Be honest about one presence habit you know you have — camera off, talking over people, poor setup, not pausing. You won't have to share it, but you should know it before you walk in.
  • Think of a time when you felt genuinely listened to in a professional setting. What did that person do differently?
  • Come dressed and set up as you would for a real prospect call — this session starts with a look at how we currently show up.
What we'll cover
  • Virtual presence: Camera on (always), eye level, lighting, background, professional dress, no multitasking. What your current setup communicates to a prospect.
  • In-person presence: How you enter a room, where you sit, posture, energy, preparation. Being the most prepared person in the room is itself a form of respect.
  • Active listening: Listening to understand, not to respond. Note-taking, mirroring language, pausing before speaking, reflecting before redirecting. We'll practise this in pairs.
  • Consultative questions: Open questions that expand, clarifying questions that deepen, confirming questions that close loops. We'll convert closed and leading questions into consultative ones as a group.
